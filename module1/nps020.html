Director's Order 36

Director's Order 36 Housing Management and Reference Manual provides guidance for administering and managing NPS employee housing (also known as Government furnished quarters).

  • The policies and procedures apply to all NPS-owned or leased housing units rented in support of Federal programs--whether rented to employees of the NPS, employees of another Interior bureau, another Federal agency, or to non-Federal tenants who are housed in order to accomplish the mission of NPS.
  • Select this link for more information on this Order:
    http://inside.nps.gov/waso/custommenu.cfm?lv=3&prg=154&id=6028
Reference Manual 36

Reference Manual 36 addresses both the administrative and facility-management aspects of the Housing Management Program, and provides parks/offices with the following:

  • Overview of regulations for acquiring, utilizing, managing, and disposing of government-furnished housing
  • Regulations for establishing and collecting rental rates and charges for related facilities
  • Guide for the implementation of the Housing Management Program
  • Quick reference guide for specific topics related to the Housing Management Program
  • Tool that offers the best practices to determine the most cost-effective and efficient use of structures
OMB Circular A-45

OMB Circular A-45 - Rental & Construction of Government Quarters:

  • Establishes rental rates and other charges for Government Quarters
  • Rents are based upon rents in area
  • Uses Appraisals and Regional Surveys
  • Considers other costs - utilities, furnishings and other services
  • Considers "Other Factors":
    • Isolation
    • Site amenities
    • Lack of Privacy
  • Establishes procedures for construction
  • Used by all executive Agencies
  • Select this link for more information on this Circular: http://www.whitehouse.gov/omb/circulars_a045
Housing Management Plan

The Housing Management Plan (HMP) serves as a park's housing framework and strategy to guide the park's housing program over the next five years. Parks have strived to make improvements in housing management and will continue to progress utilizing the information and plans laid out in the HMP.

  • This plan covers the following critical components of housing management :
    • Inventory: Determined by the park's Housing Needs Assessment
    • Required Occupancy If applicable
    • Cost to Operate
    • Condition
  • Guidance regarding tenant responsibilities and expectations as park housing occupants.
  • HMPs will reference numerous existing park sources of information:
    • General Management Plan (GMP)
    • Business Plan
    • Needs Assessment Certification
    • Park Asset Management Plan (PAMP)
    • budget data from Housing Income and Expenditure Reports
    • Facility Management Software System (FMSS) data
    • iQMIS data
    • Policy documents such as the Director's Order 36 and Reference Manual 36.
  • HMPs are meant to be managed and updated to reflect changing park conditions and inventory to ensure most efficient and effective management of critical housing resources.
