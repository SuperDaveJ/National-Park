Lesson 1: Introduction to Housing Management

Internet Quarters Management Information System (iQMIS) for Records Management

Page 1 of 14

The iQMIS is an automated inventory of government housing. Select each numbered tab below to learn more.

Ownership and Design

  • It is owned by the Department of the Interior and administered through the Quarters Management Information System (QMIS) Office in Denver, Colorado.
  • It is designed to keep records on each housing structure to include, but not limited to, location, structural features, amenities and services, and tenant history.

Use

It is maintained by house number and its uses include, but are not limited to:

  • Monthly Rental & Adjustment Calculation
  • Notice of Rental Adjustment
  • Housing Assignment Agreement
  • Certificate of Required Occupancy
  • Excessive Heating/Cooling Agreement
  • Request for Bi-weekly Quarters Deductions
  • Quarters Inventory
  • Employee Check In/Out Form
  • Lead-Based Paint Disclosure

Calculating and Adjusting Rent

iQMIS is used to calculate and adjust tenant rents. Each year in March the Consumer Price Index (CPI) is applied to the current rent.

  • Updates are provided via memorandum and posted on the web-site.
  • QMIS will calculate adjustments related to the CPI and survey results when applicable.
